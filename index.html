Authentic Hotel in Bukhara

Thirty-two rooms in the old city — five minutes on foot from Po-i-Kalyan, the Ark and Lyabi-Hauz.

01

A small hotel that belongs to the city — not the other way around.

Bukhara has been receiving travellers for two and a half thousand years. We tried to add as little to that as possible: thirty-two rooms behind a quiet door, ceilings carved by local masters, breakfast on the courtyard tiles, and a front desk that has the time to draw you a walking map by hand.

Otabek Khayyamov Owner — second generation
02

A few reasons guests return.

A central address.

The Ark, Po-i-Kalyan and Lyabi-Hauz are all reachable on foot in under fifteen minutes.

Generously sized rooms.

Even the entry-level rooms are above the city average — built for families, couples and group travellers.

A long, unhurried breakfast.

Hot bread from the courtyard oven, dried fruits, eggs to order, mountain honey and tea kept hot until eleven.

Fast Wi-Fi, everywhere.

Symmetric fibre across all rooms, the lobby, both courtyards and the roof.

A silence that has left most cities.

Every room sits behind a metre of brick. The street ends at the courtyard door.

Trusted by nine years of guests.

Consistently rated for service, comfort and a reliable, very human front desk.
